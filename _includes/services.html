What is Human-Computer Interaction?

Overview

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is an interdisciplinary field of study focusing on the design, evaluation, and implementation of interactive technology. It combines aspects from various fields including psychology, cognitive science, computer science, and engineering.

Core Components
  • User: The individual using the system.
  • Task: What the user is accomplishing using the system.
  • Tools/Interface: The programs and devices used.
  • Context: The environment in which the system is used.
Significant Milestones

Starting with the theoretical concepts in 1945 to the development of the first HCI programs in universities like Carnegie Mellon and Indiana University in the 1990s.

Current Research
  • Examining the effects of screen reading versus paper.
  • Investigating the impact of aging on interaction skills.
  • Exploring effective color combinations and user interface design.
Practical Applications

Today, HCI research influences user-centered design approaches in technology, helping to create systems that are more intuitive and better suited to users' needs.
